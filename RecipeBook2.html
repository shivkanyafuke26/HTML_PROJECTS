Dhokla

Dhokla is a savory steamed cake made from fermented rice and chickpea flour batter. It is a popular snack in India.

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour: 1 cup
  • Chickpea flour (besan): 1 cup
  • Curd (yogurt): 1/2 cup
  • Ginger-green chili paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Turmeric powder: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Eno fruit salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix rice flour, chickpea flour, curd, ginger-green chili paste, turmeric powder, and salt in a bowl to make a smooth batter.
  2. Add water if needed to adjust the consistency. The batter should be pourable but not too thin.
  3. Grease a steamer plate or a baking dish and pour the batter into it.
  4. Steam for about 15-20 minutes until the dhokla is cooked and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  5. Let it cool slightly, then cut into squares or diamonds.
  6. For tempering, heat oil in a small pan, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. Pour this tempering over the dhokla pieces.
  7. Serve dhokla with green chutney or sweet chutney.

Kheer (Khir)

Kheer, also known as Khir, is a creamy and delicious rice pudding made by simmering rice in milk and sweetened with sugar. It is flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts.

Ingredients:

  • Rice: 1/4 cup
  • Milk: 4 cups
  • Sugar: 1/2 cup
  • Cardamom powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios): for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash rice and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, boil milk over medium heat.
  3. Add soaked rice to the boiling milk and stir well.
  4. Simmer the mixture on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens.
  5. Add sugar and cardamom powder. Mix well and cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  6. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  7. Garnish with chopped nuts and serve warm or chilled.
